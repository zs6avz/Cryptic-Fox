The SVV Digital Mystery

Sodalitas Vulturis Volantis (SVV) is an online puzzle project associated with the Twitter account @svulturvolans. The account directs participants to humanisbeing.com, a website containing cryptic text, Latin phrases, and ambient audio. The project functions as an ARG-style puzzle sequence, with no confirmed purpose beyond interactive problem‑solving.

Section 1

Entry Point: The SVV Twitter Account and Website

The Twitter account @svulturvolans is active and publicly accessible. It links to humanisbeing.com, which contains:

  • Latin text
  • Symbolic imagery
  • Embedded audio
  • Links to additional puzzle pages

These elements are verifiable and form the starting point of the SVV puzzle trail.

Section 2

Application Requirements

SVV’s application process includes a set of instructions displayed on the website:

  • Applicants must solve an initial cipher.
  • They must write the solution on a handwritten note.
  • A photo of this note must be emailed to secretary@humanisbeing.com.

The site instructs applicants to use fake personal information for name and address but a real email address for correspondence. These requirements are publicly visible on the site and can be confirmed directly.

Section 3

Gatekeeper “Tyme” and Cipher Challenges

The URL /applicants contains two encrypted sentences, both of which are Vigenère ciphers. The first decrypts to: “I am Tyme, producer of cunninge.” The second requires two keys — “SVV” and “thirtythree” — to decrypt.

A subsequent page, /2-2, contains:

  • A hexadecimal string
  • A clue referencing “what’s Above”
  • A key phrase derived from the first line of the image: “followme”

These puzzle elements are verifiable by visiting the pages.

Section 4

Use of Coordinates

The SVV Twitter account has posted geographic coordinates corresponding to real-world locations. However, there is no confirmed evidence of physical clues at these sites, and no publicly verified documentation shows that objects or symbols were found there. Thus, the coordinates are factual, but their significance is unverified.

Section 5

A central role in the puzzle's internal narrative.

These roles are part of the ARG’s internal narrative; there is no evidence they correspond to real-world authority or organizational membership.

Section 6

Reported Participant Interactions

Some participants have shared accounts of interactions involving figures named Misero and TriVav, and email messages referencing task delegation. These reports are anecdotal and cannot be independently verified. No public archive or cryptographically signed evidence confirms these exchanges.

Section 7

Current Status of the SVV Puzzle

As of the latest publicly available information, no new confirmed stages have been released. Participants report reaching a point where progression is unclear. The associated domains remain online but do not display new content. The project appears to be in a paused or inactive state, though no official statement confirms this.

Conclusion

SVV is a publicly accessible online puzzle project involving a Twitter account, a series of cryptographic challenges, a website with multiple hidden pages, a fictional in‑game hierarchy, and occasional use of geographic coordinates. All verifiable elements relate to ARG mechanics, not real-world organizational activity. Unverified participant stories exist but cannot be treated as factual without supporting evidence.
